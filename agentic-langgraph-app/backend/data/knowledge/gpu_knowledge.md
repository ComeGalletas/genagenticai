## Product: NVIDIA GeForce RTX 5090

**Category:** GPU  
**Manufacturer:** NVIDIA  
**Aliases:** RTX 5090, GeForce RTX 5090

### Overview

The NVIDIA GeForce RTX 5090 is the flagship consumer graphics card of the RTX 50 Series, based on the Blackwell architecture. It is designed for enthusiast gaming, AI workloads, content creation, and professional applications requiring maximum GPU performance.

### Specifications

- Architecture: Blackwell
- VRAM: 32 GB GDDR7
- Memory Bus: 512-bit
- CUDA Cores: 21760
- TDP: 575 W
- PCI Express: PCIe 5.0

### Features

- DLSS 4
- Multi Frame Generation
- Fourth-generation Ray Tracing acceleration
- Fifth-generation Tensor Cores
- DisplayPort 2.1
- AV1 hardware encoding and decoding

### Performance

The RTX 5090 targets native 4K and 8K gaming, professional rendering, AI inference, and large generative AI models. It represents the highest level of gaming performance in the RTX 50 Series.

### Pricing

Launch MSRP: $1,999 USD

## Product: NVIDIA GeForce RTX 5050

**Category:** GPU  
**Manufacturer:** NVIDIA  
**Aliases:** RTX 5050, GeForce RTX 5050

### Overview

The RTX 5050 is the entry-level desktop GPU in the Blackwell RTX 50 Series. It is aimed at budget builds and casual gamers who want modern features such as DLSS 4 and hardware-accelerated ray tracing at an accessible price point.

### Specifications

- Architecture: Blackwell
- VRAM: 8 GB GDDR7
- Memory Bus: 128-bit
- CUDA Cores: 2560
- TDP: 130 W
- PCI Express: PCIe 5.0

### Features

- DLSS 4
- Ray Tracing (hardware accelerated)
- NVIDIA Reflex
- AV1 hardware encoder/decoder
- DisplayPort 2.1 / HDMI 2.1

### Performance

The RTX 5050 targets 1080p gaming at medium-to-high settings and provides a significant generational leap over the RTX 3050 / 4050 in rasterization and AI-assisted rendering thanks to DLSS 4 with Multi Frame Generation.

### Pricing

Launch MSRP: ~$249 USD
